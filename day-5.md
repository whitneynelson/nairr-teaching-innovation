## What you'll build today

### Publish your course site

Publishing via GitHub Pages is one way to share your finished course site, not a requirement; you can just as easily export or hand off the material to Google Classroom, Canvas, Blackboard, or whatever LMS your institution already uses.

If you do want it live on GitHub Pages: before turning anything on, do a pre-publish leak check on `course-site`; confirm no answer keys, grading detail, student data, or private prep notes made it in, and re-check your program's Three Hard Rules with your facilitator. Then: **Settings → Pages** → set source branch (usually `main`) → save. Click through every page once it's live; syllabus, schedule, modules, resources, office hours; and confirm nothing 404s and nothing private is visible.

### Office hours + LMS export

Set up your booking link (Calendly or Teams) and write a short office hours policy. Then export Module 4's slide decks and materials to Canva/Blackboard for your LMS, if your campus has Canva–Blackboard LTI enabled. LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability) is the technical handshake that lets Canva plug directly into Blackboard; if your campus has it turned on, your slides can go straight from Canva into your course page instead of being downloaded and re-uploaded by hand.

### Reuse this template

Confirm both `course-site` and `course-toolkit` are set as GitHub template repos (**Settings** → check **"Template repository"**). For your next course or cohort: your repo structure, rubric templates, and this Builder Guide carry over cleanly; your learning outcomes, curriculum map, module content, and exam questions need rebuilding each time.
